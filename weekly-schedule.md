# 10-Week Schedule

Assumed budget: `8-10 hours/week`

## Week 1
- Read `TempCompass`, `Video-MME`, `MVBench`
- Build your first pass taxonomy in the field map
- Write two full paper notes
- Write one half-page memo: "What changes when a benchmark is broad versus truly temporal?"

## Week 2
- Read `TimeChat`, `MovieChat`, `UniVTG`
- Compare timestamp-aware, memory-based, and unified-grounding views
- Add one paragraph per paper cluster on what would fail outside benchmark settings

## Week 3
- Read `MLVU`, `LongVideoBench`, `Towards Debiasing Temporal Sentence Grounding in Video`
- Focus on benchmark pathologies and long-context evaluation
- Write one synthesis memo: "How current benchmarks overestimate temporal ability"

## Week 4
- Read `Re-thinking Temporal Search for Long-Form Video Understanding`, `VITED`, `Consistency of Video LLMs in Temporal Comprehension`
- Compare search, evidence chains, and consistency as three distinct ways to expose model weakness
- Start the open-problems tracker

## Week 5
- Read `Temporal Reasoning Transfer from Text to Video`, `Seq2Time`, `ProTeGe`
- Focus on where temporal knowledge should enter: encoder, data, or LLM
- Draft `5-7` research questions in the tracker

## Week 6
- Read `STEP`, `VideoComp`, `Compositional Video Understanding with Spatiotemporal Structure-based Transformers`
- Compare explicit structure, synthetic reasoning supervision, and negative perturbation design
- Downselect to the best `4` problem statements

## Week 7
- Read `Omnia de EgoTempo`, `Motion-Grounded Video Reasoning`, `VideoEspresso`
- Focus on fine-grained temporal reasoning and grounded evidence
- Define minimal validation probes for the top `3` ideas

## Week 8
- Read `Localizing Moments in Long Video Via Multimodal Guidance`, `Scanning Only Once`, `Temporal Action Detection Robustness`
- Compare search efficiency, pruning, and robustness under corruptions
- Downselect to `2-3` candidate directions

## Week 9
- Read `VideoRefer Suite` and targeted nearest-neighbor papers around your top two ideas
- Draft one-page research briefs for each candidate direction
- Force yourself to write the weakest assumption for each direction

## Week 10
- Choose one lead direction
- Write the final research brief with problem, gap, closest prior work, minimal experiment, and success criteria
- Decide whether the next phase is:
  - benchmark probe
  - lightweight prototype
  - deeper narrow literature pass

## Weekly Deliverables
- `2-3` full paper notes
- `1` weekly synthesis note
- `1` update to the field map
- `1` update to open problems
