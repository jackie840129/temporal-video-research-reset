- Venue: TPAMI

## One-line takeaway

This survey is mainly valuable as a map of temporal grounding terminology, task variants, and open directions.

## What problem it claims to solve

It organizes the temporal sentence grounding literature so later papers can be compared without confusion about task definitions.

## Reading highlights

- The paper is a reference point for vocabulary rather than a direct temporal hallucination benchmark.
- It helps separate moment retrieval, temporal grounding, and related task variants.
- It is useful when older papers use slightly different names for similar problems.

## Section notes

### Abstract

The abstract presents the survey as a broad overview of grounding methods, datasets, and future directions.

### Introduction

The introduction is useful for understanding how the field defined temporal grounding before the current Video LLM wave.

### Method

As a survey, the method is best understood as taxonomy and literature organization rather than a new algorithm.

### Experiments

The paper is not primarily about experiments; its value is in the framing and comparative structure.

### Limitations

It predates much of the video LLM hallucination literature, so it is more of a historical anchor than a direct solution.
